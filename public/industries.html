Industries

Built for how Maldivian businesses actually work

We don't sell one template to everyone. Each industry loses customers in a different way, so each gets a different system.

Guesthouses & Stays

The leak: overseas guests enquire at 2am, get no answer, and book the guesthouse next door on Booking.com, which takes 15-18% commission.

The fix: a direct-booking website with live availability, deposit payment and automatic confirmation. Keep the commission; own the guest relationship.

Cafés & Restaurants

The leak: the menu lives in an Instagram highlight from last Ramadan, and reservation requests sit unread during the dinner rush.

The fix: an always-current menu, table reservations that confirm themselves, and a Google profile that fills tables on quiet nights.

Salons & Spas

The leak: booking by DM means double-bookings, no-shows and a stylist checking a phone between every client.

The fix: online appointment booking with time slots per staff member, deposits for premium services, and automatic reminders that cut no-shows.

Boat Charters & Water Sports

The leak: tourists plan excursions weeks ahead from another timezone. If your trips, prices and photos aren't online, you're not in their plan.

The fix: a trip catalogue with dates, prices and online booking with deposit, plus reviews that build trust before they land.

Construction & Trades

The leak: big clients Google you before awarding work. No website, no portfolio, no professional email, no shortlist.

The fix: a portfolio site that proves your track record, quotation requests that arrive structured, and a project dashboard for jobs, tasks and petty cash.

Clinics & Health Services

The leak: patients call during consultations, can't get through, and give up. Opening hours and services exist only on a printed board.

The fix: online appointment requests, clear services and hours on Google, and a patient-friendly website that answers before the phone rings.

Retail & Boutiques

The leak: "Is this available?" asked fifty times a day in DMs, answered one at a time, while stock runs out unnoticed.

The fix: a product catalogue customers browse themselves, order requests that arrive structured, and Inventory Lite so you restock before it's gone.

Professional Services

The leak: accountants, consultants and agencies win work on trust, and not having a website makes you look less established.

The fix: a credible site with services, team and results, business email on your own domain, and a client portal for documents and status.
